The S&P is holding at elevated levels but the internals look weak. Less than 40% of S&P 500 components are above their 50-day moving average even as the index itself is near highs — a classic breadth divergence.

Combined with widening high-yield spreads and weak Russell 2000 participation, I'm expecting a pullback to the 5,000 level before the next leg higher.

**Invalidation:** A close above 5,400 with improving breadth and volume would invalidate this thesis.
